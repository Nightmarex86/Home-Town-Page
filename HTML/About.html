Anfeh right now

Anfeh is a picturesque coastal town in northern Lebanon, still holding onto its traditional charm while embracing modern tourism. It’s a popular summer destination for both locals and tourists, offering a peaceful escape with its beaches, historical sites, and authentic local experiences. The town has a close-knit community and remains a key spot for olive oil production and sea salt harvesting.

History

Anfeh has a rich history, dating back to ancient times. It was once a Phoenician settlement, later becoming part of Roman and Byzantine territories. The Crusaders also had a strong presence in Anfeh, leaving behind structures like the Anfeh Citadel, a fortress built to protect the area. Throughout the centuries, the town has been an important hub for trade, maritime activity, and agriculture, especially known for its salt production and olive groves.

Culture

Anfeh is known for its rich cultural heritage, with a strong emphasis on tradition and local customs. The town has a diverse population, with a mix of Arab, Persian, and Greek-speaking residents. The town's architecture is characterized by its beautifully carved stone walls, which reflect the town's rich history and heritage. The town also has a vibrant art scene, with various art galleries and museums dedicated to local artists and artisans.

Tourism

Anfeh draws visitors for its natural beauty and historical landmarks. The beaches, especially around Tahet El Rih, are known for their crystal-clear waters and charming, white-and-blue seaside cafés and houses. Tourists also explore the ruins of the Anfeh Citadel, the Deir El Natour Monastery, and the salt pans that have been in use for centuries. It’s a quiet alternative to Lebanon’s bustling cities, offering a blend of relaxation, history, and nature.
